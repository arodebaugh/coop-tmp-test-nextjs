Handoffs and Transitions in Critical Care – Understanding Scalability (HATRICC-US)

Sponsor: National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI)

Period of performance: 9/1/20 – 6/30/25


Surgical patients who require post-surgical critical care are usually transferred from the operating room (OR) to the intensive care unit (ICU) for ongoing care. The process of transferring the patient from the OR to the ICU is called a handoff. Handoffs involve the transfer of patient information and transfer of patient care responsibilities from the OR team to the ICU team. Multiple studies suggest that these handoffs can expose patients to preventable harm, that can be avoided with handoff standardization. The aims of this study are to:
1. Ascertain determinants of OR-to-ICU handoff protocol adoption and use in 12 adult and pediatric ICUs in five health systems.
2. Adapt handoff protocols using engineering approaches and select tailored implementation strategies with implementation mapping
3. Test the effectiveness of tailored, multifaceted, multilevel implementation strategies.
4. Design and create a digital toolkit for other ICUs to identify implementation determinants, customize OR-to-
ICU handoff protocol, and select appropriate implementation strategies.



Enhanced ultrasound treatment of chronic wounds with monitoring of healing and quality of life outcomes

Sponsor: National Institute of Nursing Research (NINR)

Period of performance: 8/9/19 - 6/30/22


The goal of the research is to develop an operational concept and refined device prototype for delivering low frequency, low intensity ultrasound therapy to patients with chronic wounds and Alzheimer’s disease or Alzheimer’s disease related dementia in a home setting by family caregivers. My role is to lead the systems engineering and human factors elements including specifying the operational concept for using and maintaining the ultrasound treatment in the home.
Focus groups with patients, caregivers, homecare nurses and wound ostomy nurses as well as simulations using the ultrasound prototype will help to define the user description, operational environment, and operation scenarios.

Disseminating & Implementing Patient and Family Centered I-PASS to Improve Patient Safety

Sponsor: Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI), DI-2017C3-9232

Period of performance: 10/15/18 - 10/14/21


This project seeks to spread the Patient- and Family-Centered I-PASS program to at least 18 diverse US children’s hospitals. Patient- and Family-Centered I-PASS is an intervention to improve how patients, families, and staff communicate in hospitals. It includes changes to communication during and after family-centered rounds (FCRs). The implementation of Patient and Family Centered I-PASS should be associated with: (a) reductions in harms due to medical care, measured through family and clinician-reporting (b) improvements in family and clinician experience; (c) improvements in family and clinician-reported hospital safety culture scores; and (d) improvements in adherence with intervention processes. My role is to help the implementation team and to help study site and patient/family predictors of variation in adoption of Patient and Family Centered I-PASS across sites, in order to inform iterative improvements and future widespread dissemination.
